---
title: "The Design of Everyday Things"
author: "Don Norman"
rating: 4.4
order: 1
review: |
  This is one of the most influential books I’ve read on design, usability, and human behavior. Despite being written decades ago, many of its lessons remain surprisingly relevant today.

  What makes this book special is that it changes the way you look at the world around you. Don Norman explains why some products feel intuitive while others leave us frustrated, confused, or prone to mistakes. Through everyday examples—doors, switches, controls, appliances, and countless other objects—he demonstrates that good design isn’t about aesthetics; it’s about helping people achieve their goals effortlessly.

  One of the book’s greatest strengths is its accessibility. You don’t need to be a designer to benefit from it. Whether you’re a Product Manager, engineer, designer, or simply someone interested in how people interact with technology, the concepts are easy to understand and immediately applicable.

  The biggest takeaway for me was learning to see products through the eyes of the user. After reading this book, I found myself paying much more attention to usability, affordances, feedback, and the small design decisions that shape everyday experiences. It’s difficult to read it without starting to notice good and bad design everywhere.

  If you’re involved in building products, this book will make you think differently about UX and user-centered design. It certainly did for me.

  Would I recommend it? Absolutely. It’s an easy and enjoyable read that will change the way you think about products, design, and user experience.
