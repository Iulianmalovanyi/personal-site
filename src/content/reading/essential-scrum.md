title: "Essential Scrum: A Practical Guide to the Most Popular Agile Process"
author: "Kenneth S. Rubin"
rating: 4.0
order: 1
review: |
  If you’re looking to understand Scrum properly, this is one of the most comprehensive resources available. Unlike many Agile books that stay at a high level, Essential Scrum goes deep into the mechanics, roles, events, artifacts, and principles that make Scrum work in practice.

  What I appreciated most about this book is that it doesn’t just explain what Scrum is—it explains why certain practices exist and how they contribute to delivering value incrementally. It helped me build a much stronger understanding of Agile product development and provided useful context for many of the ceremonies and practices that teams often follow without fully understanding their purpose.

  That said, I wouldn’t describe this as a light read. The book is detailed and sometimes feels more like a reference guide than something you read cover to cover. For experienced Product Managers, some sections may feel familiar or overly prescriptive, especially if you’ve worked in organizations that have adapted Scrum to fit their own context.

  One of the key lessons I took away is that Scrum itself isn’t the goal. The goal is to create an environment where teams can learn quickly, collaborate effectively, and deliver value continuously. The framework is only useful if it helps achieve those outcomes.

  While Agile and Scrum have evolved significantly since the book was published, many of the fundamentals remain relevant today and provide a solid foundation for anyone working in product development.

  Would I recommend it? Yes. Especially for those who are new to Agile or want a deeper understanding of Scrum beyond the basic certifications and training courses. It’s one of the most complete guides on the subject.
