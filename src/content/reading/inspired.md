title: "Inspired: How to Create Tech Products Customers Love (2nd Edition)"
author: "Marty Cagan"
rating: 4.9
order: 1
review: |
  If I had to recommend a single product management book, this would be it.

  Inspired had a bigger impact on my product thinking than any other book I’ve read. I discovered it early in my product management career, and many of the ideas continue to influence how I approach products, teams, and decision-making today.

  One of the book’s most important lessons is that great products don’t start with features—they start with a deep understanding of customer problems. The goal isn’t to ship more functionality; it’s to create solutions that people genuinely need, value, and love using.

  Another idea that resonated strongly with me is the distinction between empowered product teams and feature teams. Great teams aren’t simply handed requirements and asked to deliver them. They’re trusted to understand the problem, explore options, and find the best solution while balancing customer needs, business goals, and technical constraints.

  The book also reinforced my belief in focusing on outcomes over output. Success isn’t measured by the number of features released but by the value those features create for customers and the business.

  What makes Inspired stand out is that it doesn’t just explain what product management is—it explains how successful product organizations think and operate. It provides a practical framework while remaining grounded in real-world examples.

  Many of the principles I follow today—customer-centricity, product discovery, empowered teams, and outcome-driven thinking—can be traced back to ideas I first encountered in this book.

  Would I recommend it? Without hesitation. Whether you’re an aspiring Product Manager, new to the role, or looking to strengthen your product foundations, this is one of the most valuable books you can read.
