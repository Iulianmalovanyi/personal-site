---
title: "Product Management in Practice"
author: "Matt LeMay"
rating: 4.5
order: 1
review: |
  This is one of the most practical books on product management I’ve read. Unlike many product books that focus on frameworks, processes, or idealized examples, Matt LeMay focuses on the reality of the job: ambiguity, communication, alignment, and helping teams make progress despite uncertainty.

  What I appreciated most is how grounded the book is. It doesn’t try to present product management as a role with all the answers. Instead, it highlights the importance of creating shared understanding, building trust, and helping people move in the same direction. That resonated strongly with my own experience.

  The book is particularly valuable for anyone who feels overwhelmed by the countless product frameworks and methodologies available today. Matt’s message is refreshing: product management is ultimately about people, conversations, and making informed decisions together.

  One idea that stayed with me is that product managers often create the most value not through ownership or authority, but through clarity and alignment. In many ways, the role is about helping teams navigate complexity and uncertainty rather than controlling outcomes.

  The writing is concise, practical, and easy to follow. It’s not a book filled with groundbreaking new concepts, but that’s exactly its strength. It focuses on the fundamentals that matter every day.

  Would I recommend it? Absolutely. Especially for aspiring and early-career Product Managers, but even experienced PMs will find useful reminders about what the role is really about. It’s a book I wish I had read earlier in my product journey.
