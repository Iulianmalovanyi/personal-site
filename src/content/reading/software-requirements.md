title: "Software Requirements (3rd Edition)"
author: "Karl Wiegers & Joy Beatty"
rating: 4.0
order: 2
review: |
  This is one of the most comprehensive books I’ve come across on requirements engineering. It’s not the kind of book I would describe as an easy read, nor is it one I read cover to cover. Instead, I’ve often treated it as a reference guide—something to return to whenever I need deeper insight into a particular aspect of requirements gathering, analysis, or documentation.

  What makes this book valuable is the level of detail. It covers the entire requirements process, from understanding stakeholder needs and defining scope to documenting, validating, and managing requirements throughout a product’s lifecycle. If you’re looking for practical guidance on how to write better requirements or structure product documentation, it’s difficult to find a more thorough resource.

  While some sections can feel dense, that’s also its strength. The book doesn’t oversimplify the topic and provides a depth of knowledge that’s hard to get from shorter articles, blog posts, or training courses.

  I wouldn’t necessarily recommend it as a first product management book, but for Product Managers, Business Analysts, and anyone responsible for defining product requirements, it’s an excellent resource to have on the shelf.

  Would I recommend it? Yes. Not necessarily as a cover-to-cover read, but definitely as a reference book. If you have questions about requirements, chances are you’ll find a well-thought-out answer somewhere in these pages.
