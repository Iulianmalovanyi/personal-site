title: "The Mom Test"
author: "Rob Fitzpatrick"
rating: 4.7
order: 1
review: |
  This is one of those books that every Product Manager should read early in their career.

  The Mom Test focuses on one of the most important aspects of product development: talking to customers and learning from them without accidentally leading them to the answers you want to hear. The core idea is simple but powerful—people are often polite, optimistic, and supportive, which can make feedback unreliable if you’re asking the wrong questions.

  The book provides a practical framework for conducting customer interviews and product discovery conversations that uncover real problems, behaviors, and needs rather than opinions or hypothetical future intentions. It teaches you how to move beyond questions like “Would you use this?” and instead understand what people actually do, what frustrates them, and what they’re willing to change.

  What I appreciate most is how actionable the advice is. After reading it, you can immediately improve the quality of your customer conversations. Many of the techniques have become part of how I approach discovery, stakeholder discussions, and user interviews today.

  The book is short, practical, and easy to apply. While the ideas themselves aren’t complicated, they can fundamentally change how you gather evidence and validate assumptions.

  Would I recommend it? Absolutely. If customer discovery, user interviews, or problem validation are part of your role, this book is essential reading. It’s one of the most practical and valuable books on product discovery I’ve come across.
